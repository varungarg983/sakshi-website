Interactive Assessment

Business Sale Readiness Check

Answer a short set of practical questions and get an instant view of how ready your business may be for sale, where the likely weak spots are, and what to work on next.

This tool is designed for New Zealand business owners thinking about selling in the next 6 to 24 months.

Before you start

This is a practical readiness check, not a formal valuation. It is intended to help owners think more clearly about preparation, buyer expectations, and timing.

Current affiliation: Sakshi Aggarwal works with Clyth McLeod. Any live version of this tool should use your approved enquiry and follow-up process.

How scoring works

For each question, choose the answer that best reflects your current situation. The score updates only when you submit the full assessment.

Yes = 2 points Your business appears to be in good shape on that item.
Partly = 1 point There is something in place, but it may need tightening up.
No = 0 points This is likely a weakness or missing area to address.

1. Owner readiness

Clarity on timing and goals makes the whole sale process easier to manage.

Do you have a clear reason for selling and a rough timeframe?
Are decision-makers aligned on the plan, including any partners or family members involved?

2. Financial readiness

Buyers want clean numbers they can understand and trust.

Are your financial statements up to date and reasonably easy to explain?
Can you clearly separate business expenses from personal or one-off items?
Are GST, PAYE, and other key obligations current?

3. Operational readiness

Businesses that depend less on the owner are usually easier to sell.

Can the business run day to day without relying on you for every key decision?
Are your core processes documented or easy to hand over?

4. Revenue and customer quality

Buyers will look closely at stability, concentration risk, and how repeatable the income is.

Is your revenue spread across more than one or two major customers?
Can you clearly explain recent sales trends and where customers come from?

5. Team, legal, and timing

This last section covers continuity, compliance, and whether your timing is right.

Would the business keep operating well if a key staff member left?
Are key contracts, leases, licences, or permits current and available where relevant?
Would going to market now be sensible, or have you largely resolved the obvious issues first?
